Alcohol Service Hours

Alcohol cannot be served or sold between midnight and 9:00 a.m. The legal drinking age on Taniti is 18.

Home/Getting Here & Around
Transportation

Getting to Taniti

Almost all visitors arrive by air. A small cruise ship also docks weekly. Once here, getting around is easy and affordable.

✈️
Airport Expansion Coming Soon

Taniti's airport currently accommodates small jets and propeller planes. An expansion to accept larger commercial jets is underway and expected within the next few years.

✈️

By Air

Most visitors fly into Taniti's small airport, which serves small jets and propeller aircraft. The primary arrival method for tourists.

🚢

By Cruise Ship

A small cruise ship docks once per week at Yellow Leaf Bay harbor for an overnight stay. Check cruise itineraries for Taniti port calls.

Getting Around the Island

Taniti City is flat and very walkable. Merriton Landing is also easy to explore on foot. For the rest of the island, several options are available.

🚌

Public Bus

Public buses serve Taniti City from 5:00 a.m. to 11:00 p.m. every day. Affordable and reliable for city travel.

🚐

Private Bus

Private bus services connect the rest of the island beyond Taniti City. Good option for day trips to attractions.

🚕

Taxis

Taxis are available throughout Taniti City and can be hailed or pre-booked. Convenient for shorter point-to-point trips.

🚗

Rental Car

A local rental agency near the airport offers car rentals. Best option for families and those wanting maximum flexibility.

🚲

Bike Rental

Bikes and helmets are available from several vendors around the island. Helmets are required by law. Great for exploring Taniti City.

🚶

On Foot

Taniti City is flat and very walkable. Merriton Landing on Yellow Leaf Bay is also easy to explore entirely on foot.

Practical Information

Travel Tips & FAQs

Everything you need to know before you arrive. Tap any question to expand the answer (multiple sections can stay open at once).

Taniti uses the U.S. dollar (USD) as its primary currency. Many businesses also accept euros and yen. Several banks on the island facilitate currency exchange for other currencies. Most businesses accept major credit cards (Visa, Mastercard, AmEx). It is always wise to carry some cash for smaller vendors and local markets.
The legal drinking age on Taniti is 18 years old. Alcohol may not be served or sold between midnight and 9:00 a.m. The drinking age is not strictly enforced, but visitors are still advised to respect local laws and customs.
Power outlets on Taniti use 120 volts, the same standard as in the United States. Standard U.S. plugs (Type A/B) work without any adapter. Visitors from other countries may need a voltage converter and plug adapter for their devices.
The official language of Taniti is Tanitian. However, many younger Tanitians speak fluent English, especially in tourist areas such as Taniti City and Merriton Landing. In rural areas, particularly with older residents, very little English may be spoken. Learning a few basic Tanitian phrases is appreciated and welcomed by locals.
Taniti has one hospital located in Taniti City, which has multilingual staff available to assist international visitors. There are also several medical clinics throughout the island for minor health needs. Travelers with specific medical requirements are advised to bring necessary prescriptions and medication with them.
Taniti is generally a very safe destination. Violent crime is very rare. However, as tourism has grown, there have been more reports of pickpocketing and other petty crimes in tourist areas. Visitors are advised to keep valuables secured, be aware of their surroundings, and avoid leaving belongings unattended on beaches. Use the hotel safe when available.
Taniti observes a large number of national holidays. Many tourist attractions, restaurants, and shops will be closed on public holidays. Visitors are strongly encouraged to check the holiday calendar before planning excursions and to book activities and dining reservations in advance to avoid disappointment.
Taniti City is flat and very walkable. Merriton Landing is also easy to explore on foot. For the rest of the island, rental cars are the most flexible option. Bike rentals are available from several vendors: helmets are required by law. Public buses run from 5 a.m. to 11 p.m. daily in Taniti City.
